Intelbras

Investors focused on understanding the dynamics of Intelbras's core security segment, the ongoing portfolio simplification in TIC, the long-term growth levers across its different verticals, and the company's capital allocation priorities going forward.

  • In the security segment, management commented that the operating environment remains challenging in the short term, with the broader demand recovery yet to materialize and B2B projects taking longer to be approved. Despite this, the company expects 2026 to compare favorably to 2025, with categories such as access control gaining more traction. On pricing, Intelbras continues to pass through costs gradually, particularly in light of the recent increase in copper-related component costs. Going forward, the key leading indicators to monitor for a more structural pickup in security demand are families' purchasing power, the construction activity index, and the trajectory of interest rates, which should ultimately translate into higher residential and logistics condominium deliveries.
  • On the TIC division, management commented that the portfolio simplification initiatives remain ongoing. GPON — which we estimate to represent ~15% of TIC sales — should continue to lose relevance, with the company expected to decide later this year whether to fully discontinue the business. In parallel, cables and corporate networks have been growing but not at a pace sufficient to offset the GPON downsize. Although gross margin in cables is structurally lower than in GPON, the EBITDA dynamics are similar — supported by the absence of post-sales costs and a strong industrial partner on the cables side. For the year, the outlook is more constructive, also helped by the ongoing anti-dumping measures.
  • Beyond its core categories, management discussed potential long-term growth levers. In the energy business, the EV charger segment has been gaining traction — Intelbras already supplies chargers to Geely and Renault — with the installation service representing a relevant avenue to improve gross margins. Additionally, the company has been testing models with installers operating in condominiums to address this opportunity. In security, the monetization of the software ecosystem — with recurring revenues and AI-enabled features (e.g., human detection) — represents another long-term lever, with higher attached value per customer.
  • On margins, management commented that an ~14% EBITDA margin appears to be a healthy and sustainable level. Regarding working capital, the company sees room for improvement (current accounts payable/inventory ratio at ~70% vs ~80% historically), although the seasonal nature of the business — which requires inventory build-up in 1H to support 2H peak sales — limits more structural changes. As for capital allocation, Intelbras has ~BRL 700 million in net cash and is generating additional cash, with no restrictions to distribute. From the discussions, an extraordinary dividend distribution in 2H26 emerged as a possibility, although management has historically been cautious on this front, while the Board has been less open to share buybacks.

Locaweb

Investors focused on understanding the operational evolution of Locaweb over the past year, the dynamics of the Brazilian marketplace landscape and the reasons behind the company's growth pace, the AI initiatives currently underway, and management's view on the future of e-commerce.

  • Management highlighted the relevant structural changes implemented over the past year — including the reorganization with more independence across business units — which have already started to translate into operational improvements. The 1Q26 results reflected better monetization of the subscription base, with the company having reached a healthier balance between profitability and growth. Bling has consolidated itself as the leading e-commerce orchestrator in Brazil, with the integration across Tray, logistics and financial services becoming increasingly seamless. Furthermore, logistics has emerged as a relevant component for client convenience and a meaningful evolution lever for the platform.
  • On the marketplace landscape, management commented that a more competitive 3P environment is favorable for Locaweb. TikTok Shop has been growing rapidly and already represents the third-largest marketplace within the company's GMV base, while Shopee continues to perform well and Amazon has resumed growth in 2H25 following relevant adjustments to better address the Brazilian market (notably faster client approval processes and product/process improvements). When asked why Locaweb's growth has not matched the pace of these marketplaces, management explained that mix dynamics play a relevant role, given the company's lower exposure to categories that have been outperforming — such as pharmacy and white goods.
  • On AI, management commented that initiatives are progressing across two main fronts: product experience and operational cost. On the product side, AI has been embedded to facilitate client experience — including reducing friction in the onboarding process and simplifying site setup — with promising early indicators (new clients completing onboarding ~30% faster, and time-to-first-sale reduced by ~15%). These improvements should translate into lower client mortality and higher LTV over time. On the cost side, AI-driven automation has already enabled a ~50% reduction in customer service headcount with simultaneous NPS improvement, with additional efficiency gains expected from operational and repetitive processes going forward.
  • Looking ahead, management sees LLMs and logistics expertise as critical differentials for the future of e-commerce. SMB clients are expected to continue purchasing through marketplaces, social commerce, and increasingly through LLMs, which should also accelerate traffic to D2C channels. However, the company believes that conversion outside of the D2C site in an LLM-driven world will take time to materialize, given the complexity of the logistics side — an area where Tray's optimization capabilities represent a key competitive advantage. Furthermore, management commented that the Brazilian e-commerce landscape is more comparable to the US and Europe than to China, given the relevance of brand equity and open payment platforms (e.g., PIX).

TIM

Investors focused on the dynamics of the competitive environment, which started the year in a more aggressive tone, and on TIM's strategy to address the prepaid segment. Management also discussed the drivers of recent bad debt evolution, the leasing initiatives, the broadband expansion strategy, and the potential implications of the Brazilian tax reform.

  • The competitive environment has felt more aggressive at the beginning of 2026, with dynamics largely related to peers' movements rather than TIM's own positioning. The year started with more aggressive offers from Nubank, followed by muscular responses from Vivo and Claro, in parallel with accelerating broadband initiatives. In this context, the backdrop is currently less constructive for a price-up in the control segment, while in pure postpaid the back-book has been fully repriced for 2026 (front-book price-up still pending). As for prepaid, the gap to control has widened materially (ARPU stagnant at ~BRL 30 vs BRL 60-65 in control), which management plans to address through portfolio actions — including the gradual removal of WhatsApp (an indirect price-up) and the recently announced PicPay partnership, expected to improve the quality of the customer base.
  • On bad debt, the recent variation reflects three drivers: i) the natural cycle of migrating customers from prepaid to control, which tends to take ~6 months to normalize; ii) the postpaid price-up executed in a less constructive backdrop; and iii) a specific B2B item related to the A2P (app-to-person) business, where a key reseller has been under pressure — a dynamic likely to persist in the coming quarters. As for leasing, TIM is pursuing two initiatives to mitigate the natural upward pressure: i) a sizable deal — comparable to the previous ATC transaction — already commercially agreed and expected to be announced in September; and ii) a more aggressive renegotiation triggered by the cancellation of a major contract. Overall, management remains optimistic about leasing cost dynamics throughout the year.
  • Regarding broadband, TIM intends to adopt a more offensive stance, capitalizing on the still-low penetration in São Paulo and on the recent network ownership change, which provides additional flexibility. With ~80% of in-store traffic comprising existing TIM customers, the retail footprint represents a relevant channel to accelerate broadband sales, with the company prioritizing organic growth in the short term. As for M&A, management commented that it appears less likely in the near term, considering that TIM is already a well-positioned pure-mobile player and that a potential transaction could compromise its dividend payment capacity.
  • On the tax reform, management commented that the 2027 price-up will need to be higher to absorb the increase in PIS and COFINS. The expectation is that suppliers will pass through the additional burden, requiring TIM to decide whether to add this on top of the regular adjustments. Regarding the overall P&L, management does not anticipate a material negative effect, although the timing for tax credit recovery — which depends on suppliers' payments and the split-billing mechanics — remains a topic to monitor. From a free cash flow perspective, no relevant impact is expected.

TOTVS

Investors focused on understanding TOTVS's positioning amid the rapid evolution of AI in enterprise software, with limited discussion on near-term fundamentals. Management addressed the long-term implications of AI for the ERP industry, TOTVS's competitive moats, the current monetization stage of its AI initiatives, the operating momentum of the core business, and the integration progress on recent acquisitions.

  • Management reinforced its bullish view on the long-term expansion of TOTVS's addressable market driven by AI. The opportunity was framed through three layers: the application itself, the cloud environment (an addressable market unlocked over the past 15 years), and the tasks layer — significantly larger than both — which should become an addressable market as agents gradually replace portions of the human workforce on integrated corporate processes. As for competitive advantages, TOTVS highlighted that ~1/3 of Brazil's GDP runs through its applications, providing data scale and sector expertise that are difficult for new entrants to replicate. Management does not see structural threats from large LLM providers nor from new startups, given the high level of specialization required for ERP-related processes.
  • Although TOTVS is not yet monetizing AI agents directly — given that the underlying technology has not reached the required levels of accuracy and reliability for critical corporate processes — the company has been generating revenues from what management calls "AI enablers". The most important enabler is the universalization of cloud, which is a prerequisite for clients to benefit from AI in terms of security, governance, and cost competitiveness. More recently, TOTVS launched an IaaS offering on top of its existing PaaS, leveraging its ~25-30% spare capacity (no additional capex required at this stage). On AI itself, management highlighted the recently launched Lynn — its specialized AI foundation, composed of an agent builder, an orchestration layer, and a governance/security layer — as the framework that should support the future "task-as-a-service" business model.
  • On the operational front, TOTVS reported strong 1Q26 results, with sequential growth and acceleration in new ARR additions of high quality (largely driven by volumes in new sales). EBITDA margin in the management segment expanded ~200bps YoY, crossing the 30% mark for the first time. The recurring revenue dynamics — which have now sustained double-digit growth for 28 consecutive quarters — should continue to support solid operational performance going forward, although it is important to note that 1Q tends to be the quarter with the most favorable seasonality of the year.
  • On M&A, management commented that the Linx integration is still at an early stage, with TOTVS having taken charge only two months ago. The current focus is on strengthening culture before pursuing the relevant revenue synergies. The strategic rationale lies in nearly full complementarity between the two companies' coverage matrices (verticals x client sizes) — for instance, TOTVS dominates supermarkets while Linx leads in gas stations — with cross- and up-sell opportunities for horizontal products (such as HR) into Linx's retail-focused base seen as the main growth lever ahead.

Vivo

As expected, the competitive environment was the main topic of discussion in the meetings, with investors focused on the dynamics of recent price-up movements across mobile and fiber, as well as the potential for repricing in prepaid throughout the year. Management also addressed Vivo's growth levers beyond core connectivity, the long-term outlook for its fiber footprint — including potential M&A scenarios — and capital allocation priorities in light of ongoing cost-saving initiatives and the company's 100% payout policy.

  • On mobile, Vivo highlighted that ~75% of the postpaid base has already been repriced, while in fiber, part of the price-up was executed in January, with the remainder scheduled for 2H26. Management commented that there is room for repricing in prepaid — where ARPU has been stagnant at ~BRL 30 — but reinforced that Vivo does not intend to be the first mover, given its non-leading position in the segment. The main competitive concern remains the potential entry of a regional player offering low-cost or bundled mobile plans. Furthermore, the migration from prepaid to control plans continues to progress with no material deterioration in bad debt in the B2C segment, while a recent variation in PDD was explained by a specific B2B integrator client (ex this client, PDD would have been 1.88% vs the 1.9% reported).
  • Beyond core connectivity, Vivo discussed several growth levers that should support its top-line evolution going forward. On the OTT front, the partnership business is approaching BRL 1 billion in revenues, and the upcoming expiration of Perplexity's exclusivity contract opens room for additional AI partnerships (e.g., Claude and OpenAI). Additionally, management is optimistic about IoT and consumer electronics sales through the retail footprint, where Vale Saúde — a discount platform for medical services — has also been gaining traction as a relevant long-term opportunity. Cloud is another growth pillar, with Vivo leveraging strong partnerships with Microsoft, Google Cloud (post-IPnet acquisition) and, increasingly, Oracle. Finally, fintech and credit initiatives represent additional monetization avenues without compromising bad debt dynamics.
  • As for fiber, Vivo reiterated the significant growth runway ahead, with its ~19% market share well below the ~35% observed in fiber leaders in developed economies. To reach this full potential, management acknowledged that M&A is likely to play an important role, with network quality and limited overlap with Vivo's existing footprint as key criteria. Furthermore, more transformational scenarios — such as a potential acquisition of V.tal, which holds the second-largest fiber network in Brazil — were also discussed, although valuation remains a critical variable. Such a deal would effectively consolidate the industry into a duopoly structure with Claro, while also unlocking revenue synergies (notably the ability to cross-sell mobile from day one) and potential value embedded in V.tal's backbone.
  • On the cost side, Vivo addressed three relevant topics. First, the bad debt dynamic in 1Q26 was driven by a specific B2B client and does not reflect any structural deterioration in the portfolio. Second, AI is expected to support efficiency gains in the medium term, with the company already developing orchestrating agents focused on plan changes, technical support, and billing inquiries — targeting a 70% retention rate on these interactions, with go-live expected between May and June. Third, the gradual removal of legacy copper assets should unlock additional operating efficiencies and savings, with copper disposal also expected to accelerate in 2Q26 amid favorable pricing and reduced tax burden. Regarding capital allocation, management reaffirmed Vivo's commitment to its 100% payout policy, noting that any potential upside from a favorable Fistel resolution at the Supreme Court could further expand the dividend base.
